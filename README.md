IoT-Based Smart Wildlife Intrusion Detection and Crop Protection System

Overview

This project is an IoT-based smart agriculture protection system developed to reduce crop damage caused by wild animals in rural and forest-border farming areas. The system uses an ESP32 microcontroller along with PIR motion sensors and IR sensors to detect animal movement near farm boundaries. Once intrusion is detected, the system activates LEDs and buzzers to scare away animals and sends alerts to farmers through WiFi communication.

The project is designed specifically for Indian agricultural conditions where farmers require low-cost, efficient, and easy-to-maintain smart farm protection systems.

---

Problem Statement

Farmers in many parts of India suffer severe crop losses due to intrusion of wild animals such as wild boars, elephants, monkeys, and deer. Traditional protection methods like fencing and manual guarding are inefficient, labor-intensive, and unsafe during nighttime. Existing commercial monitoring systems are costly and not affordable for small-scale farmers.

This project aims to provide an affordable and automated wildlife intrusion detection system for rural agriculture.

Working Principle

1. PIR sensor detects movement near farm boundaries.
2. IR sensor confirms intrusion detection.
3. ESP32 processes the sensor data.
4. LEDs and buzzers activate automatically.
5. Alert notification is sent to the farmer through WiFi.
